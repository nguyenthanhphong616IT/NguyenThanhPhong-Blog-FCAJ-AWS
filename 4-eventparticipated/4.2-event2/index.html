Event 2

FCAJ Community Day 23-05

Purpose of the Event

  • Better understand how AI and Cloud are changing the way we learn, work, and build technology products.
  • Through practical sharing, I realized that AI is only truly effective when there is clear context, the right mindset, and appropriate application.
  • The event also helped me access more knowledge about prompts, CloudFront, Multi-Agent, data analysis, and product development in real-world environments.
  • Gain more orientation to train professional skills, self-learning skills, and problem-solving mindset in the AI era.

List of Speakers

  • Mr. Tinh - Platform Engineer at GoTymeX
  • Pham Nguyen Hai Anh - G-AsiaPacific Vietnam
  • Nguyen Tuan Thinh - DevOps Engineer at First Cloud AI Journey Vietnam
  • Ms. Thao (VIB Team) - VIB
  • Mr. Dao Duc - Solution Architect at Cloud Kinetics Vietnam
  • Ms. Cat Vy - Senior Business Systems Analyst at VPBank

Highlights

1. Speaker Mr. Tinh – Platform Engineer at GoTymeX

Topic: Context Is Everything: Making AI Actually Work for You

Mr. Tinh shared about the critical role of context when working with AI. The main content emphasized that AI often gives poor results not because the tool is weak, but because the user has not provided enough information, goals, background data, and specific requirements.

The presentation also explained the development of AI from simply inputting basic prompts to models capable of remembering, understanding work history, and supporting in the direction of a Second AI Brain. When AI has enough context, the output will be more accurate, closer to the needs, and can better support learning, research, as well as practical work.

In addition, Mr. Tinh shared career perspectives, helping students understand how to start building products with AI, how to train problem-solving mindsets, and how to use AI as a long-term support tool.

Results or values achieved: Through this sharing, I understand that when using AI, it is necessary to provide full context, goals, and related data instead of just asking short questions. I learned how to think more clearly when working with AI, how to improve prompt quality, and gained more orientation to apply AI in learning, project making, and career skill development.

2. Speaker Pham Nguyen Hai Anh - G-AsiaPacific Vietnam

Topic: Friendly AI Assistant with Amazon Quick

Speaker Pham Nguyen Hai Anh introduced how to use Amazon Quick to build AI assistants supporting data analysis, information extraction, and workflow automation. The presentation focused on helping users interact with data using natural language, reducing the need to write complex code.

Main contents included Quick Chat Agent, a tool that helps users ask questions, explore data, and analyze insights; Quick Flows, allowing the creation of smart workflows using natural language without programming; Quick Spaces, supporting collaborative spaces to turn individual understanding into shared team knowledge; and Quick Sight, helping to build dashboards and reports from raw data through natural language commands.

The presentation helped participants better visualize how AI can support businesses in data analysis, task automation, and improving collaboration among team members.

Results or values achieved: From this sharing, I learned how AI can support faster data analysis and report building through natural language. I also understood more about creating no-code workflows, organizing team knowledge, and applying AI tools in data processing, dashboard creation, or supporting decision-making in projects.

3. Speaker Nguyen Tuan Thinh – DevOps Engineer at First Cloud AI Journey Vietnam

Topic: From Edge To Origin: CloudFront as Your Foundation

Mr. Nguyen Tuan Thinh presented about Amazon CloudFront and its role in content delivery, performance optimization, increasing reliability, and protecting systems when deploying applications on the cloud.

Main contents included how CloudFront can be applied to various types of workloads, from static websites, web applications, APIs to systems requiring global content delivery. He also shared about cost optimization capabilities when using CloudFront, thanks to offloading the origin server and utilizing edge locations.

Besides, the presentation also mentioned security capabilities such as access control, combining with network and application layer protection services, while enhancing system reliability and performance through caching, reducing latency, and improving the end-user experience.

Results or values achieved: Through this sharing, I better understood the role of Amazon CloudFront in modern cloud architecture. I learned how CloudFront helps accelerate websites, reduce server load, optimize costs, improve security, and enhance system stability. This knowledge can be applied to web projects, API systems, or applications needing to serve users in multiple regions.

4. Speaker Team VIB Ms. Thao - VIB International Bank

Topic: 36 hrs with LotusHacks – Building UTMorpho from Idea to Reality

Team VIB shared their journey participating in the LotusHacks hackathon and the process of building the UTMorpho product within 36 hours. The presentation started with the reasons the team joined the competition, the brainstorming process to find ideas, identify problems to solve, and shape the product.

The sharing emphasized the working process under short time pressure, where the team had to quickly assign tasks, develop the product, handle errors, adjust directions, and complete the demo. Team VIB also shared their difficulties, failures, and critical turning points in the process of turning an idea into a real product.

In addition, the team provided an overview of UTMorpho, presented the product demo, and drew lessons learned after the competition, including teamwork, time management, problem-solving, and product development in a hackathon environment.

Results or values achieved: From this presentation, I learned how an idea can be developed into a product in a short time through effective teamwork. I understood more about the brainstorming process, identifying problems, building solutions, delegating work, and handling pressure when participating in a hackathon. This is useful experience to apply to group projects, tech competitions, or real-world projects.

5. Speaker Dao Duc – Solution Architect at Cloud Kinetics

Topic: Non-Determinism of “Deterministic” LLM Settings

Mr. Dao Duc presented how large language models select the next token when generating responses. The main content helped participants understand that LLM results depend not only on the prompt but also on how the model handles probabilities, inference systems, and backend technical optimizations.

An important point in the sharing is the common assumption that Temperature = 0 will always produce completely fixed results. However, in reality, optimizations during inference can still cause results to vary in some cases. This creates a practical impact when using LLMs for systems requiring stability, testing, automation, or critical data processing.

The speaker also shared risk mitigation strategies such as prompt control, fixing configurations, multiple evaluations, using guardrails, result logging, and designing systems capable of double-checking AI output.

Results or values achieved: Through this sharing, I gained a deeper understanding of how LLMs generate responses and why AI sometimes still yields different results despite identical configurations. I learned to view AI more realistically, knowing the need for testing, monitoring, and building control mechanisms when integrating LLMs into projects. This knowledge helps me be more careful when using AI in problems requiring high accuracy and stability.

6. Speaker Cat Vy – Senior Business Systems Analyst at VPBank

Topic: Enterprise-Grade Multi-Agent System: The Case of Startup Credit Scoring

Ms. Cat Vy shared about building a Multi-Agent AI system in an enterprise environment, through the problem of credit scoring for startups. The presentation started with the difference between traditional banking systems and startup data, as startups often lack a complete financial history, have inconsistent data, and are difficult to evaluate using standard credit models.

She analyzed when to use a Single Agent and when to switch to Multi-Agent. For complex problems with multiple data sources and evaluation criteria, a Multi-Agent model can simulate a Virtual Credit Committee, where each agent assumes a specific role such as financial analysis, risk assessment, compliance checking, summarizing insights, and making recommendations.

The presentation also emphasized the role of guardrails and compliance in the banking sector to ensure the AI system operates safely, transparently, and in accordance with regulations. In addition, she mentioned operational ROI, deployment roadmaps, and the future development direction of Multi-Agent systems in enterprises.

Results or values achieved: From this sharing, I learned how AI can be applied to complex enterprise problems, especially in the banking and credit evaluation sector. I better understood the Multi-Agent model, how to assign roles to each agent, how to control risks with guardrails, and the importance of compliance when deploying AI in a real-world environment. This content helped me expand my mindset on applying AI not only in programming but also in business analysis and enterprise system design.
